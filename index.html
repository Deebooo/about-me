CLIP-BOT: AI-Powered Chatbot for Splunk Log Monitoring

Overview:

Implementation of an AI-powered chatbot transforms how users interact with Splunk by allowing natural language queries instead of requiring knowledge of SPL, Splunk's query language. It simplifies log analysis, making it intuitive and accessible for everyone.


Key Points:

  • Implementation of streamlined log monitoring and metric analysis, making the process intuitive and accessible to non-technical users through natural language queries.
  • Facilitation of multi-agent collaboration using advanced AI systems for task-specific automation and coordination.
  • Enhancement of operational efficiency through the automation of query generation and response formulation.
  • Delivery of an improved user experience by providing accurate, actionable insights in real time.
  • Integration of long-term memory to ensure continuity and context-aware interactions.

Tech Stack: Python, Dataiku, Splunk, LangChain, LangGraph, LangSmith, Streamlit

Personal Projects

Colorization of Historical Aerial Images

Overview:

Implementation of a GenAI approach to bring historical aerial imagery from 1945 to 2003 to life in realistic color. This project leverages the power of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) to colorize grayscale satellite imagery, transforming archival data into visually rich and accessible resources for historical research and geographic analysis.


Key Points:

  • Utilization of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) to achieve high-quality and realistic colorization of black-and-white aerial images.
  • Adoption of the LAB colorspace for precise colorization, preserving original structural details while enhancing chromatic accuracy.
  • Optimization of computational workflows with NVIDIA GPUs, CUDA acceleration, and distributed training for high-resolution image processing.
  • Evaluation of results using metrics like PSNR for detail preservation and CIEDE2000 for color accuracy based on human perception.
  • Contribution to historical urban planning and geographic exploration by enabling visualization of land use changes and urban development over time.

Tech Stack: Python, PyTorch, CUDA, OpenCV, rasterio, Pillow, skimage

Satellite Image Segmentation for Historical Urban Landscape Reconstruction

Overview:

Implementation of deep learning models to reconstruct historical urban landscapes and analyze the impact of soil impermeabilization on urban overheating. By leveraging historical orthophotos and satellite data, this project provides insights into urbanization patterns and their environmental consequences, aiding decision-making for sustainable urban planning.


Key Points:

  • Development of U-Net-based segmentation model to classify land surfaces as permeable or impermeable using historical orthophotos.
  • Evaluation of urbanization's impact on surface temperatures by correlating segmentation results with satellite temperature data.
  • Quantification of the effect of soil impermeabilization on urban overheating over time, with an observed increase of up to 6°C in some areas.
  • Integration of AI and geomatics for environmental analysis and urban planning applications.

Tech Stack: Python, PyTorch, CUDA, OpenCV, rasterio, Pillow, skimage
